    Career Counselling

  • Career counselling helps individuals explore career options, set goals, and make choices that align with their interests and skills.
  • Some career counselling services are free, while others may have fees. It depends on the provider and the level of service.

    • College Counselling

  • College counselling is a service that assists students in the college application process, including choosing schools, preparing applications, and financial aid.
  • College counselling can begin as early as high school, but it's most common during the junior and senior years to prepare for college applications.
  • Practice answering common interview questions, research the college, and dress professionally for the interview.

    • Financial Aid

  • Financial aid options include scholarships, grants, loans, and work-study programs. Start by completing the FAFSA to determine your eligibility.
  • Scholarships are typically merit-based and awarded for academic achievements or specific talents. Grants, on the other hand, are need-based and provided to students based on their financial circumstances. Both can help offset the cost of education.
